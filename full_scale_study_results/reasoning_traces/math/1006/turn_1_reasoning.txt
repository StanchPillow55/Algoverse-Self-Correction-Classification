To solve this problem, we need to calculate the volume of the swimming pool, convert that volume into gallons, and then determine the cost to fill the pool based on the given price per gallon.

### Step 1: Calculate the Volume of the Pool

The pool is rectangular, so we can use the formula for the volume of a rectangular prism:

\[
\text{Volume} = \text{length} \times \text{width} \times \text{depth}
\]

Given:
- Length = 25 feet
- Width = 14 feet
- Depth = 4 feet

Substitute these values into the formula:

\[
\text{Volume} = 25 \, \text{ft} \times 14 \, \text{ft} \times 4 \, \text{ft} = 1400 \, \text{cubic feet}
\]

### Step 2: Convert Cubic Feet to Gallons

The problem states that to convert the volume in cubic feet to gallons, we multiply by 5.9:

\[
\text{Gallons} = 1400 \, \text{cubic feet} \times 5.9 = 8260 \, \text{gallons}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Cost to Fill the Pool

The cost to fill the pool is $0.10 per gallon. Therefore, we multiply the number of gallons by the cost per gallon:

\[
\text{Cost} = 8260 \, \text{gallons} \times 0.10 \, \text{dollars per gallon} = 826 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Final Answer

The cost to fill the pool is \(\boxed{826 \, \text{dollars}}\).
