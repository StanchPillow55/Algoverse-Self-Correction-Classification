To solve this problem, we need to determine the cost per pair of contacts when Pete buys 2 boxes, taking into account the discount.

### Step 1: Determine the Total Number of Contacts
Each box contains 90 single-use contacts. If Pete buys 2 boxes, the total number of contacts is:
\[ 90 \text{ contacts/box} \times 2 \text{ boxes} = 180 \text{ contacts} \]

### Step 2: Calculate the Total Cost with Discount
The original price of one box is $100.00. With a 10% discount, the price of one box becomes:
\[ \text{Discount} = 10\% \times 100 = 10 \]
\[ \text{Discounted price per box} = 100 - 10 = 90 \]

For 2 boxes, the total cost is:
\[ 90 \text{ dollars/box} \times 2 \text{ boxes} = 180 \text{ dollars} \]

### Step 3: Calculate the Cost per Pair of Contacts
Since each contact is single-use, a pair of contacts consists of 2 contacts. The number of pairs in 180 contacts is:
\[ \frac{180 \text{ contacts}}{2 \text{ contacts/pair}} = 90 \text{ pairs} \]

Now, calculate the cost per pair:
\[ \text{Cost per pair} = \frac{180 \text{ dollars}}{90 \text{ pairs}} = 2 \text{ dollars/pair} \]

### Final Answer
The cost per pair of contacts is $2.00.
