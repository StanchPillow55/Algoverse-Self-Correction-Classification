**Hypothetical Problem:**

A farmer has a rectangular field that is 150 meters long and 100 meters wide. He wants to divide the field into two equal parts by constructing a fence parallel to the shorter side. How long will the fence be, and what will be the area of each part?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - The field is rectangular with dimensions 150 meters (length) and 100 meters (width).
   - The farmer wants to divide the field into two equal parts with a fence parallel to the shorter side (100 meters).

2. **Visualize the Problem:**
   - Imagine the rectangle with the longer side (150 meters) as the base.
   - The fence will run parallel to the shorter side (100 meters), effectively dividing the rectangle into two smaller rectangles.

3. **Determine the Length of the Fence:**
   - Since the fence is parallel to the shorter side, its length will be equal to the length of the longer side of the rectangle.
   - Therefore, the length of the fence is 150 meters.

4. **Calculate the Area of Each Part:**
   - The total area of the field is given by the formula for the area of a rectangle: 
     \[
     \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width} = 150 \, \text{m} \times 100 \, \text{m} = 15,000 \, \text{m}^2
     \]
   - Since the field is divided into two equal parts, each part will have half of the total area:
     \[
     \text{Area of each part} = \frac{15,000 \, \text{m}^2}{2} = 7,500 \, \text{m}^2
     \]

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The length of the fence required to divide the field into two equal parts is 150 meters.
   - The area of each part of the field after division is 7,500 square meters.

**Final Answer:**
- Length of the fence: 150 meters
- Area of each part: 7,500 square meters
