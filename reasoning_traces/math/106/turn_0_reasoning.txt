To find the total inner volume of all 3 boxes, we first need to determine the inner dimensions of a single box, taking into account the thickness of the walls.

### Step 1: Determine the outer dimensions of a box
The outer dimensions of each box are given as:
- Length = 5 inches
- Width = 6 inches
- Height = 4 inches

### Step 2: Calculate the inner dimensions
Since the walls of the box are 1 inch thick, we need to subtract twice the thickness of the walls from each dimension to find the inner dimensions. This is because the thickness affects both sides of each dimension.

- **Inner Length**:
  \[
  \text{Inner Length} = \text{Outer Length} - 2 \times \text{Wall Thickness} = 5 - 2 \times 1 = 5 - 2 = 3 \text{ inches}
  \]

- **Inner Width**:
  \[
  \text{Inner Width} = \text{Outer Width} - 2 \times \text{Wall Thickness} = 6 - 2 \times 1 = 6 - 2 = 4 \text{ inches}
  \]

- **Inner Height**:
  \[
  \text{Inner Height} = \text{Outer Height} - 2 \times \text{Wall Thickness} = 4 - 2 \times 1 = 4 - 2 = 2 \text{ inches}
  \]

### Step 3: Calculate the inner volume of a single box
The inner volume \( V \) of a box can be calculated using the formula:
\[
V = \text{Inner Length} \times \text{Inner Width} \times \text{Inner Height}
\]
Substituting the inner dimensions we calculated:
\[
V = 3 \text{ inches} \times 4 \text{ inches} \times 2 \text{ inches}
\]
Calculating this:
\[
V = 3 \times 4 = 12 \text{ square inches}
\]
\[
V = 12 \times 2 = 24 \text{ cubic inches}
\]

### Step 4: Calculate the total inner volume for all 3 boxes
Since there are 3 identical boxes, we multiply the inner volume of one box by 3:
\[
\text{Total Inner Volume} = 3 \times \text{Volume of one box} = 3 \times 24 \text{ cubic inches}
\]
Calculating this:
\[
\text{Total Inner Volume} = 72 \text{ cubic inches}
\]

### Final Answer
The total inner volume of all 3 boxes is \( \boxed{72} \) cubic inches.
