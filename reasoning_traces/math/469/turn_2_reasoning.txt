To determine how much Jackson spends on champagne to fill the hot tub, we need to follow these steps:

1. **Convert the hot tub capacity from gallons to quarts**:
   - The hot tub holds 40 gallons.
   - Since there are 4 quarts in a gallon, we can calculate the total quarts:
     \[
     40 \text{ gallons} \times 4 \text{ quarts/gallon} = 160 \text{ quarts}
     \]

2. **Determine the number of bottles of champagne needed**:
   - Each bottle of champagne holds 1 quart, so Jackson needs:
     \[
     160 \text{ quarts} \div 1 \text{ quart/bottle} = 160 \text{ bottles}
     \]

3. **Calculate the total cost without discount**:
   - Each bottle costs $50, so the total cost for 160 bottles is:
     \[
     160 \text{ bottles} \times 50 \text{ dollars/bottle} = 8000 \text{ dollars}
     \]

4. **Apply the 20% volume discount**:
   - A 20% discount means Jackson pays 80% of the total cost. First, we calculate 20% of $8000:
     \[
     0.20 \times 8000 = 1600 \text{ dollars}
     \]
   - Now, subtract this discount from the total cost:
     \[
     8000 \text{ dollars} - 1600 \text{ dollars} = 6400 \text{ dollars}
     \]

5. **Final answer**:
   - Therefore, the total amount Jackson spends on champagne is:
     \[
     \boxed{6400 \text{ dollars}}
     \]
