**Example Problem:**
A train travels from City A to City B at a speed of 60 km/h and returns at a speed of 40 km/h. What is the average speed for the entire journey?

**Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - We need to find the average speed for the round trip.
   - The train travels two different speeds for equal distances.

2. **Plan a Strategy:**
   - Use the formula for average speed: \(\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}\).

3. **Execute the Plan:**
   - Let the distance from City A to City B be \(d\).
   - Time taken from A to B: \(t_1 = \frac{d}{60}\).
   - Time taken from B to A: \(t_2 = \frac{d}{40}\).
   - Total distance for the round trip: \(2d\).
   - Total time for the round trip: \(t_1 + t_2 = \frac{d}{60} + \frac{d}{40}\).

   To add the times, find a common denominator:
   \[
   t_1 + t_2 = \frac{d}{60} + \frac{d}{40} = \frac{2d}{120} + \frac{3d}{120} = \frac{5d}{120} = \frac{d}{24}
   \]

   - Average speed:
   \[
   \text{Average Speed} = \frac{2d}{\frac{d}{24}} = 2d \times \frac{24}{d} = 48 \text{ km/h}
   \]

4. **Review and Conclude:**
   - The calculations are consistent and correct.
   - The average speed for the entire journey is \(48 \text{ km/h}\).

**Final Answer:**
The average speed for the entire journey is 48 km/h.
