**Sample Problem:**

A train travels from City A to City B, a distance of 240 miles. The train travels the first half of the distance at a speed of 60 miles per hour and the second half at a speed of 40 miles per hour. What is the average speed of the train for the entire trip?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - Total distance = 240 miles.
   - First half distance = 240 / 2 = 120 miles at 60 mph.
   - Second half distance = 120 miles at 40 mph.
   - We need to find the average speed for the entire trip.

2. **Calculate Time for Each Half:**
   - Time for the first half = Distance / Speed = 120 miles / 60 mph = 2 hours.
   - Time for the second half = 120 miles / 40 mph = 3 hours.

3. **Calculate Total Time:**
   - Total time = Time for first half + Time for second half = 2 hours + 3 hours = 5 hours.

4. **Calculate Average Speed:**
   - Average speed is defined as Total Distance / Total Time.
   - Total distance = 240 miles.
   - Total time = 5 hours.
   - Average speed = 240 miles / 5 hours = 48 mph.

5. **Verify the Solution:**
   - The train covers 240 miles in 5 hours, which confirms the average speed calculation.

**Final Answer:**

The average speed of the train for the entire trip is 48 miles per hour.
