To solve the problem, we need to calculate the total water usage for each farmer based on the crops they grow and the amount of water each crop requires. Then, we will find out what percentage of the total water used goes to Farmer Bob's farm.

### Step 1: Calculate the water usage for each farmer

**Water requirements:**
- Corn: 20 gallons/acre
- Cotton: 80 gallons/acre
- Beans: 2 * Corn = 2 * 20 = 40 gallons/acre

**Farmer Bob:**
- Corn: 3 acres
- Cotton: 9 acres
- Beans: 12 acres

Calculating water usage for Farmer Bob:
- Water for corn: \( 3 \text{ acres} \times 20 \text{ gallons/acre} = 60 \text{ gallons} \)
- Water for cotton: \( 9 \text{ acres} \times 80 \text{ gallons/acre} = 720 \text{ gallons} \)
- Water for beans: \( 12 \text{ acres} \times 40 \text{ gallons/acre} = 480 \text{ gallons} \)

Total water for Farmer Bob:
\[
60 + 720 + 480 = 1260 \text{ gallons}
\]

**Farmer Brenda:**
- Corn: 6 acres
- Cotton: 7 acres
- Beans: 14 acres

Calculating water usage for Farmer Brenda:
- Water for corn: \( 6 \text{ acres} \times 20 \text{ gallons/acre} = 120 \text{ gallons} \)
- Water for cotton: \( 7 \text{ acres} \times 80 \text{ gallons/acre} = 560 \text{ gallons} \)
- Water for beans: \( 14 \text{ acres} \times 40 \text{ gallons/acre} = 560 \text{ gallons} \)

Total water for Farmer Brenda:
\[
120 + 560 + 560 = 1240 \text{ gallons}
\]

**Farmer Bernie:**
- Corn: 2 acres
- Cotton: 12 acres

Calculating water usage for Farmer Bernie:
- Water for corn: \( 2 \text{ acres} \times 20 \text{ gallons/acre} = 40 \text{ gallons} \)
- Water for cotton: \( 12 \text{ acres} \times 80 \text{ gallons/acre} = 960 \text{ gallons} \)

Total water for Farmer Bernie:
\[
40 + 960 = 1000 \text{ gallons}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the total water usage for all farmers

Now we sum the total water usage for all three farmers:
\[
\text{Total water} = \text{Water for Bob} + \text{Water for Brenda} + \text{Water for Bernie}
\]
\[
\text{Total water} = 1260 + 1240 + 1000 = 3500 \text{ gallons}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the percentage of total water used by Farmer Bob

To find the percentage of the total water used that goes to Farmer Bob's farm, we use the formula:
\[
\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Water for Bob}}{\text{Total water}} \right) \times 100
\]
Substituting the values:
\[
\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{1260}{3500} \right) \times 100
\]

Calculating the fraction:
\[
\frac{1260}{3500} = 0.36
\]

Now, converting to percentage:
\[
0.36 \times 100 = 36\%
\]

### Final Answer
The percentage of the total water used that goes to Farmer Bob's farm is \( \boxed{36\%} \).
