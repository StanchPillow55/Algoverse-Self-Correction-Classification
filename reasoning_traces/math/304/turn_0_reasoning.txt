To solve this problem, we need to calculate the total amount of water used by each farmer and then determine what percentage of the total water is used by Farmer Bob.

### Step 1: Calculate Water Usage for Each Crop

1. **Corn**:  
   - Water required per acre = 20 gallons

2. **Cotton**:  
   - Water required per acre = 80 gallons

3. **Beans**:  
   - Water required per acre = 2 × 20 gallons = 40 gallons

### Step 2: Calculate Water Usage for Each Farmer

**Farmer Bob:**
- Corn: 3 acres × 20 gallons/acre = 60 gallons
- Cotton: 9 acres × 80 gallons/acre = 720 gallons
- Beans: 12 acres × 40 gallons/acre = 480 gallons

Total water for Farmer Bob = 60 + 720 + 480 = 1260 gallons

**Farmer Brenda:**
- Corn: 6 acres × 20 gallons/acre = 120 gallons
- Cotton: 7 acres × 80 gallons/acre = 560 gallons
- Beans: 14 acres × 40 gallons/acre = 560 gallons

Total water for Farmer Brenda = 120 + 560 + 560 = 1240 gallons

**Farmer Bernie:**
- Corn: 2 acres × 20 gallons/acre = 40 gallons
- Cotton: 12 acres × 80 gallons/acre = 960 gallons

Total water for Farmer Bernie = 40 + 960 = 1000 gallons

### Step 3: Calculate Total Water Usage

Total water used by all farmers = 1260 (Bob) + 1240 (Brenda) + 1000 (Bernie) = 3500 gallons

### Step 4: Calculate Percentage of Total Water Used by Farmer Bob

Percentage of total water used by Farmer Bob = \(\frac{\text{Water used by Farmer Bob}}{\text{Total water used by all farmers}} \times 100\)

\[ 
= \frac{1260}{3500} \times 100 
\]

\[ 
= \frac{1260 \times 100}{3500} 
\]

\[ 
= \frac{126000}{3500} 
\]

\[ 
= 36\%
\]

### Final Answer

Farmer Bob uses 36% of the total water.
