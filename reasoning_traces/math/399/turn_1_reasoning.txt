To solve the problem, we need to determine how long it takes Tony to assemble one widget. We will first calculate the number of widgets assembled by Sam and Jack, and then use the total number of widgets assembled to find Tony's rate.

### Step 1: Calculate Sam's Contribution
Sam can assemble 1 widget every 10 minutes. In 6 hours, he worked for:
\[
6 \text{ hours} = 6 \times 60 = 360 \text{ minutes}
\]
The number of widgets Sam can assemble in 360 minutes is:
\[
\text{Widgets by Sam} = \frac{360 \text{ minutes}}{10 \text{ minutes/widget}} = 36 \text{ widgets}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate Jack's Contribution
When Jack helps, they assemble 2 widgets every 15 minutes. In 4 hours, Jack worked for:
\[
4 \text{ hours} = 4 \times 60 = 240 \text{ minutes}
\]
The number of 15-minute intervals in 240 minutes is:
\[
\frac{240 \text{ minutes}}{15 \text{ minutes}} = 16 \text{ intervals}
\]
Thus, the number of widgets Jack can help assemble is:
\[
\text{Widgets by Jack} = 2 \text{ widgets/interval} \times 16 \text{ intervals} = 32 \text{ widgets}
\]
