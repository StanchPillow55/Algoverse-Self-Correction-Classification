**Problem:**
A train travels from City A to City B, which is 300 miles apart. The train travels the first half of the distance at a speed of 60 miles per hour and the second half at a speed of 40 miles per hour. What is the average speed of the train for the entire trip?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - Total distance from City A to City B = 300 miles.
   - The distance is divided into two equal halves: 150 miles each.
   - Speed for the first half = 60 miles per hour.
   - Speed for the second half = 40 miles per hour.
   - We need to find the average speed for the entire trip.

2. **Calculate the Time Taken for Each Half:**
   - **First Half:**
     \[
     \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{150 \text{ miles}}{60 \text{ miles per hour}} = 2.5 \text{ hours}
     \]
   - **Second Half:**
     \[
     \text{Time} = \frac{150 \text{ miles}}{40 \text{ miles per hour}} = 3.75 \text{ hours}
     \]

3. **Calculate the Total Time for the Trip:**
   \[
   \text{Total Time} = 2.5 \text{ hours} + 3.75 \text{ hours} = 6.25 \text{ hours}
   \]

4. **Calculate the Average Speed:**
   - Average speed is defined as the total distance divided by the total time.
   \[
   \text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{300 \text{ miles}}{6.25 \text{ hours}} = 48 \text{ miles per hour}
   \]

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The average speed of the train for the entire trip is 48 miles per hour.

**Final Answer:**
The average speed of the train for the entire trip is 48 miles per hour.
