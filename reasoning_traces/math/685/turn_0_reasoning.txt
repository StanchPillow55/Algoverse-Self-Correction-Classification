To find out how much Catherine pays in total at the grocery store, we need to calculate the cost of each type of fruit she buys and then sum those amounts.

1. **Cost of Apples**:
   - Catherine buys 1 kilo of apples for $4.
   - Therefore, the total cost for apples is:
     \[
     \text{Cost of Apples} = 1 \, \text{kilo} \times 4 \, \text{dollars/kilo} = 4 \, \text{dollars}
     \]

2. **Cost of Bananas**:
   - Catherine buys 2 kilos of bananas at a price of $2 per kilo.
   - Therefore, the total cost for bananas is:
     \[
     \text{Cost of Bananas} = 2 \, \text{kilos} \times 2 \, \text{dollars/kilo} = 4 \, \text{dollars}
     \]

3. **Cost of Oranges**:
   - Catherine buys 2 kilos of oranges at a price of $3 per kilo.
   - Therefore, the total cost for oranges is:
     \[
     \text{Cost of Oranges} = 2 \, \text{kilos} \times 3 \, \text{dollars/kilo} = 6 \, \text{dollars}
     \]

4. **Total Cost**:
   - Now, we add the costs of all the fruits together to find the total amount Catherine pays:
     \[
     \text{Total Cost} = \text{Cost of Apples} + \text{Cost of Bananas} + \text{Cost of Oranges}
     \]
     \[
     \text{Total Cost} = 4 \, \text{dollars} + 4 \, \text{dollars} + 6 \, \text{dollars} = 14 \, \text{dollars}
     \]

Thus, the total amount Catherine pays at the grocery store is **14 dollars**. 

Final answer: **14 dollars**.
