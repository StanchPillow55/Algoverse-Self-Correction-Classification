**Problem:**
A farmer has a rectangular field with a length that is twice its width. The area of the field is 2000 square meters. What are the dimensions of the field?

**Solution:**

1. **Define Variables:**
   - Let \( w \) be the width of the field in meters.
   - Since the length is twice the width, the length \( l \) can be expressed as \( l = 2w \).

2. **Write the Equation for Area:**
   - The area \( A \) of a rectangle is given by the formula \( A = \text{length} \times \text{width} \).
   - Substituting the known values, we have:
     \[
     A = l \times w = 2w \times w = 2w^2
     \]
   - We know the area is 2000 square meters, so:
     \[
     2w^2 = 2000
     \]

3. **Solve for \( w \):**
   - Divide both sides of the equation by 2 to isolate \( w^2 \):
     \[
     w^2 = \frac{2000}{2} = 1000
     \]
   - Take the square root of both sides to solve for \( w \):
     \[
     w = \sqrt{1000}
     \]
   - Simplify \( \sqrt{1000} \):
     \[
     w = \sqrt{100 \times 10} = \sqrt{100} \times \sqrt{10} = 10\sqrt{10}
     \]

4. **Calculate the Length:**
   - Since the length \( l \) is twice the width:
     \[
     l = 2w = 2 \times 10\sqrt{10} = 20\sqrt{10}
     \]

5. **Verify the Solution:**
   - Check if the calculated dimensions satisfy the area condition:
     \[
     A = l \times w = (20\sqrt{10}) \times (10\sqrt{10}) = 200 \times 10 = 2000 \text{ square meters}
     \]
   - The calculations confirm that the area is indeed 2000 square meters.

**Final Answer:**
The dimensions of the field are \( 10\sqrt{10} \) meters for the width and \( 20\sqrt{10} \) meters for the length.
