To determine how much Janet owes in total for her employees' wages and taxes for one month, we will break down the problem into several steps:

### Step 1: Calculate the monthly wages for warehouse workers

1. **Number of warehouse workers**: 4
2. **Hourly wage for warehouse workers**: $15/hour
3. **Hours worked per day**: 8 hours
4. **Days worked per month**: 25 days

**Monthly wage for one warehouse worker**:
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = \text{Hourly wage} \times \text{Hours per day} \times \text{Days per month}
\]
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = 15 \, \text{dollars/hour} \times 8 \, \text{hours/day} \times 25 \, \text{days/month}
\]
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = 15 \times 8 \times 25 = 3000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

**Total monthly wage for all warehouse workers**:
\[
\text{Total wage for warehouse workers} = \text{Monthly wage} \times \text{Number of warehouse workers}
\]
\[
\text{Total wage for warehouse workers} = 3000 \, \text{dollars} \times 4 = 12000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the monthly wages for managers

1. **Number of managers**: 2
2. **Hourly wage for managers**: $20/hour

**Monthly wage for one manager**:
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = \text{Hourly wage} \times \text{Hours per day} \times \text{Days per month}
\]
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = 20 \, \text{dollars/hour} \times 8 \, \text{hours/day} \times 25 \, \text{days/month}
\]
\[
\text{Monthly wage} = 20 \times 8 \times 25 = 4000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

**Total monthly wage for all managers**:
\[
\text{Total wage for managers} = \text{Monthly wage} \times \text{Number of managers}
\]
\[
\text{Total wage for managers} = 4000 \, \text{dollars} \times 2 = 8000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate total wages for all employees

**Total wages for all employees**:
\[
\text{Total wages} = \text{Total wage for warehouse workers} + \text{Total wage for managers}
\]
\[
\text{Total wages} = 12000 \, \text{dollars} + 8000 \, \text{dollars} = 20000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Step 4: Calculate FICA taxes

FICA taxes are 10% of the total wages.

**FICA tax calculation**:
\[
\text{FICA taxes} = 0.10 \times \text{Total wages}
\]
\[
\text{FICA taxes} = 0.10 \times 20000 \, \text{dollars} = 2000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Step 5: Calculate total amount owed

**Total amount owed (wages + FICA taxes)**:
\[
\text{Total amount owed} = \text{Total wages} + \text{FICA taxes}
\]
\[
\text{Total amount owed} = 20000 \, \text{dollars} + 2000 \, \text{dollars} = 22000 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Final Answer

Janet owes a total of **$22,000** for her employees' wages and taxes for one month.
