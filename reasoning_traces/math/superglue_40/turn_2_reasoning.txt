**Hypothetical Problem:**
A farmer has a rectangular field with a length that is 10 meters longer than its width. The area of the field is 200 square meters. What are the dimensions of the field?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Define Variables:**
   - Let \( w \) be the width of the field in meters.
   - Then the length of the field is \( w + 10 \) meters.

2. **Set Up the Equation:**
   - The area of a rectangle is given by the formula: 
     \[
     \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width}
     \]
   - Substitute the known values into the equation:
     \[
     200 = (w + 10) \times w
     \]

3. **Expand and Simplify the Equation:**
   - Distribute \( w \) across the terms in the parentheses:
     \[
     200 = w^2 + 10w
     \]

4. **Rearrange the Equation:**
   - Move all terms to one side to form a quadratic equation:
     \[
     w^2 + 10w - 200 = 0
     \]

5. **Solve the Quadratic Equation:**
   - Use the quadratic formula, where \( a = 1 \), \( b = 10 \), and \( c = -200 \):
     \[
     w = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
     \]
   - Calculate the discriminant:
     \[
     b^2 - 4ac = 10^2 - 4 \times 1 \times (-200) = 100 + 800 = 900
     \]
   - Since the discriminant is a perfect square, proceed with the quadratic formula:
     \[
     w = \frac{-10 \pm \sqrt{900}}{2}
     \]
     \[
     w = \frac{-10 \pm 30}{2}
     \]

6. **Find the Possible Values for \( w \):**
   - Calculate the two potential solutions:
     \[
     w = \frac{-10 + 30}{2} = \frac{20}{2} = 10
     \]
     \[
     w = \frac{-10 - 30}{2} = \frac{-40}{2} = -20
     \]

7. **Interpret the Solutions:**
   - Since a width cannot be negative, discard \( w = -20 \).
   - Therefore, the width \( w \) is 10 meters.

8. **Calculate the Length:**
   - Substitute \( w = 10 \) back into the expression for the length:
     \[
     \text{Length} = w + 10 = 10 + 10 = 20 \text{ meters}
     \]

**Final Answer:**
The dimensions of the field are 10 meters in width and 20 meters in length.
