**Hypothetical Problem:**

A farmer has a rectangular field that is 100 meters long and 50 meters wide. He wants to divide this field into smaller rectangular plots, each with an area of 250 square meters. How many such plots can the farmer create?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - We have a rectangular field with dimensions 100 meters by 50 meters.
   - The total area of the field is \(100 \times 50 = 5000\) square meters.
   - Each plot should have an area of 250 square meters.
   - We need to find out how many 250 square meter plots can fit into the 5000 square meter field.

2. **Calculate the Number of Plots:**
   - To find the number of plots, we divide the total area of the field by the area of one plot.
   - Number of plots = \(\frac{\text{Total area of the field}}{\text{Area of one plot}}\)
   - Number of plots = \(\frac{5000}{250}\)

3. **Perform the Division:**
   - \(\frac{5000}{250} = 20\)

   - \(20 \times 250 = 5000\)
   - The calculation is correct.

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The farmer can create 20 plots, each with an area of 250 square meters, from the field.

The farmer can create 20 plots.
