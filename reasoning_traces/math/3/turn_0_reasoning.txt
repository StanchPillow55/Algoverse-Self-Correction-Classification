To find out how many total meters James runs in a week, we can break the problem down into smaller steps.

1. **Determine the distance James runs in one sprint**: 
   James runs 60 meters in each sprint.

2. **Calculate the total distance for one session of sprints**: 
   James runs 3 sprints in one session. Therefore, the total distance for one session is:
   \[
   \text{Distance per session} = \text{Number of sprints} \times \text{Distance per sprint}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   \[
   \text{Distance per session} = 3 \text{ sprints} \times 60 \text{ meters/sprint} = 180 \text{ meters}
   \]

3. **Calculate the total distance for a week**: 
   James runs 3 sessions a week. Therefore, the total distance he runs in a week is:
   \[
   \text{Total distance per week} = \text{Distance per session} \times \text{Number of sessions per week}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   \[
   \text{Total distance per week} = 180 \text{ meters/session} \times 3 \text{ sessions/week} = 540 \text{ meters}
   \]

4. **Final answer**: 
   Thus, the total distance James runs in a week is:
   \[
   \boxed{540} \text{ meters}
   \]
