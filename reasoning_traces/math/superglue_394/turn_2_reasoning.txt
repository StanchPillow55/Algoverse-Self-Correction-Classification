**Problem:**  
A farmer has a rectangular field that is 150 meters long and 100 meters wide. He wants to divide this field into smaller rectangular plots, each with an area of 1000 square meters. How many such plots can he create?

**Step-by-step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - We have a rectangular field with dimensions 150 meters by 100 meters.
   - We need to divide this field into smaller plots, each with an area of 1000 square meters.

2. **Calculate the Total Area of the Field:**
   - The area of a rectangle is given by the formula: Area = Length × Width.
   - For the farmer's field: Area = 150 meters × 100 meters = 15,000 square meters.

3. **Determine the Number of Plots:**
   - Each plot has an area of 1000 square meters.
   - To find the number of plots, divide the total area of the field by the area of one plot.
   - Number of plots = Total Area of Field / Area of One Plot
   - Number of plots = 15,000 square meters / 1000 square meters = 15 plots.

4. **Verify the Solution:**
   - Multiply the number of plots by the area of one plot to ensure it equals the total area of the field.
   - 15 plots × 1000 square meters/plot = 15,000 square meters.
   - This matches the total area of the field, confirming our solution is correct.

5. **State the Final Answer:**
   - The farmer can create 15 plots, each with an area of 1000 square meters.

By following these steps, we have reasoned through the problem, performed the necessary calculations, and verified our solution. The final answer is that the farmer can create 15 plots.
