**Sample Problem:**
A train travels from City A to City B, a distance of 180 miles. The train travels the first half of the distance at a speed of 60 miles per hour and the second half at a speed of 90 miles per hour. What is the average speed of the train for the entire trip?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - Total distance = 180 miles.
   - First half distance = 180/2 = 90 miles at 60 mph.
   - Second half distance = 90 miles at 90 mph.
   - We need to find the average speed for the entire trip.

2. **Calculate Time for Each Segment:**
   - Time for the first half:
     \[
     \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{90 \text{ miles}}{60 \text{ mph}} = 1.5 \text{ hours}
     \]

   - Time for the second half:
     \[
     \text{Time} = \frac{90 \text{ miles}}{90 \text{ mph}} = 1 \text{ hour}
     \]

3. **Total Time for the Trip:**
   - Total time = Time for the first half + Time for the second half
   \[
   \text{Total time} = 1.5 \text{ hours} + 1 \text{ hour} = 2.5 \text{ hours}
   \]

4. **Calculate Average Speed:**
   - Average speed is given by the total distance divided by the total time.
   \[
   \text{Average speed} = \frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{Total time}} = \frac{180 \text{ miles}}{2.5 \text{ hours}} = 72 \text{ mph}
   \]

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The average speed of the train for the entire trip is 72 miles per hour.

**Final Answer:**
The average speed of the train for the entire trip is 72 mph.
