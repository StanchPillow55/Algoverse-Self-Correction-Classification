2. **Break Down the Problem:**
   - Let the distance from Station A to Station B be \(d\).

3. **Apply Logical Reasoning:**
   - Time taken to travel from A to B = \(\frac{d}{60}\) hours.
   - Time taken to travel from B to A = \(\frac{d}{40}\) hours.
   - Total distance = \(2d\).
   - Total time = \(\frac{d}{60} + \frac{d}{40}\).

4. **Perform Calculations:**
   - Find a common denominator for the times: \(\frac{d}{60} + \frac{d}{40} = \frac{2d}{120} + \frac{3d}{120} = \frac{5d}{120} = \frac{d}{24}\).

   - Average speed = \(\frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{Total time}} = \frac{2d}{\frac{d}{24}} = 2d \times \frac{24}{d} = 48\) km/h.
