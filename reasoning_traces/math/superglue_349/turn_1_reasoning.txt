**Problem Statement:**
A company is evaluating two projects, Project A and Project B. Project A requires an initial investment of $100,000 and is expected to generate a return of $120,000 after one year. Project B requires an initial investment of $150,000 and is expected to generate a return of $180,000 after one year. Assuming no other costs and a discount rate of 5%, which project should the company choose based on the Net Present Value (NPV)?

**Solution:**

1. **Understand the NPV Formula:**
   \[
   \text{NPV} = \sum \left( \frac{R_t}{(1 + r)^t} \right) - C_0
   \]
   Where:
   - \( R_t \) is the net cash inflow during the period \( t \),
   - \( r \) is the discount rate,
   - \( t \) is the number of time periods,
   - \( C_0 \) is the initial investment.

2. **Calculate NPV for Project A:**
   - Initial investment (\( C_0 \)) = $100,000
   - Cash inflow after one year (\( R_1 \)) = $120,000
   - Discount rate (\( r \)) = 5% or 0.05
   - Time period (\( t \)) = 1 year

   \[
   \text{NPV}_A = \frac{120,000}{(1 + 0.05)^1} - 100,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_A = \frac{120,000}{1.05} - 100,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_A = 114,285.71 - 100,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_A = 14,285.71
   \]

3. **Calculate NPV for Project B:**
   - Initial investment (\( C_0 \)) = $150,000
   - Cash inflow after one year (\( R_1 \)) = $180,000

   \[
   \text{NPV}_B = \frac{180,000}{(1 + 0.05)^1} - 150,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_B = \frac{180,000}{1.05} - 150,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_B = 171,428.57 - 150,000
   \]
   \[
   \text{NPV}_B = 21,428.57
   \]

4. **Compare the NPVs:**
   - NPV of Project A = $14,285.71
   - NPV of Project B = $21,428.57

5. **Conclusion:**
   Since the NPV of Project B is higher than that of Project A, the company should choose Project B.

**Final Answer:**
The company should choose Project B, as it has a higher NPV of $21,428.57 compared to Project A's NPV of $14,285.71.
