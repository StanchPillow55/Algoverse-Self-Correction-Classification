**Hypothetical Problem:**
A farmer has a rectangular field that is 100 meters long and 60 meters wide. He wants to divide the field into two equal areas by constructing a fence parallel to one of the sides. How long will the fence be?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - The field is rectangular with dimensions 100 meters (length) and 60 meters (width).
   - We need to divide the field into two equal areas using a fence parallel to one of the sides.

2. **Calculate the Total Area of the Field:**
   - Area = Length × Width = 100 meters × 60 meters = 6000 square meters.

3. **Determine the Area of Each Half:**
   - Since the field is to be divided into two equal areas, each half will have an area of:
   - Half Area = Total Area / 2 = 6000 square meters / 2 = 3000 square meters.

4. **Decide the Orientation of the Fence:**
   - The fence can be parallel to either the length or the width. Let's consider both scenarios.

5. **Scenario 1: Fence Parallel to the Length (100 meters):**
   - If the fence is parallel to the length, it will divide the width.
   - Let the new width be \( x \).
   - Area of one half = Length × New Width = 100 meters × \( x \) = 3000 square meters.
   - Solve for \( x \):
     \[
     100x = 3000 \\
     x = \frac{3000}{100} = 30 \text{ meters}
     \]
   - The fence will be 30 meters long.

6. **Scenario 2: Fence Parallel to the Width (60 meters):**
   - If the fence is parallel to the width, it will divide the length.
   - Let the new length be \( y \).
   - Area of one half = New Length × Width = \( y \) × 60 meters = 3000 square meters.
   - Solve for \( y \):
     \[
     60y = 3000 \\
     y = \frac{3000}{60} = 50 \text{ meters}
     \]
   - The fence will be 50 meters long.

7. **Conclusion:**
   - The farmer has two options for constructing the fence:
     - A 30-meter fence parallel to the length.
     - A 50-meter fence parallel to the width.

**Final Answer:**
The fence can be either 30 meters long if parallel to the length or 50 meters long if parallel to the width.
