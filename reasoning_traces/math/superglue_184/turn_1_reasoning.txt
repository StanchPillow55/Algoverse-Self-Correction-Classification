**Hypothetical Problem:**

A farmer has a rectangular field that is 100 meters long and 60 meters wide. He wants to divide the field into two equal parts by constructing a fence parallel to the shorter side. How long will the fence be, and what will be the area of each part?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - The field is a rectangle with a length of 100 meters and a width of 60 meters.
   - The farmer wants to divide the field into two equal parts by constructing a fence parallel to the shorter side (width).

2. **Visualize the Problem:**
   - Imagine the rectangle with dimensions 100m (length) x 60m (width).
   - The fence will be parallel to the 60m side, meaning it will run along the length of the field.

3. **Determine the Length of the Fence:**
   - Since the fence is parallel to the shorter side (60m), it will span the entire length of the field.
   - Therefore, the length of the fence will be equal to the length of the field, which is 100 meters.

4. **Calculate the Area of Each Part:**
   - The total area of the field is given by the formula for the area of a rectangle: 
     \[
     \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width} = 100 \, \text{m} \times 60 \, \text{m} = 6000 \, \text{m}^2
     \]
   - Since the field is divided into two equal parts, each part will have an area of:
     \[
     \text{Area of each part} = \frac{6000 \, \text{m}^2}{2} = 3000 \, \text{m}^2
     \]

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The length of the fence required is 100 meters.
   - The area of each part of the field is 3000 square meters.

**Final Answer:**
- Length of the fence: 100 meters
- Area of each part: 3000 square meters
