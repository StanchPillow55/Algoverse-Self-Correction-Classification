A farmer has a rectangular field that is 100 meters long and 50 meters wide. He wants to divide the field into two equal parts by building a fence parallel to the shorter side. Calculate the length of the fence needed and the area of each part after the division.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Understand the Problem:**
   - The field is rectangular with dimensions 100 meters (length) and 50 meters (width).
   - The farmer wants to divide the field into two equal parts with a fence parallel to the shorter side (width).

2. **Visualize the Division:**
   - Since the fence is parallel to the shorter side (50 meters), it will run along the length of the field.
   - This means the fence will be 100 meters long, as it spans the entire length of the field.

3. **Calculate the Length of the Fence:**
   - The length of the fence needed is equal to the length of the field, which is 100 meters.

4. **Calculate the Area of Each Part:**
   - The total area of the field is given by the formula for the area of a rectangle: 
     \[
     \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width} = 100 \, \text{m} \times 50 \, \text{m} = 5000 \, \text{m}^2
     \]
   - Since the field is divided into two equal parts, each part will have an area of:
     \[
     \text{Area of each part} = \frac{5000 \, \text{m}^2}{2} = 2500 \, \text{m}^2
     \]

5. **Conclusion:**
   - The length of the fence needed is 100 meters.
   - The area of each part after the division is 2500 square meters.
