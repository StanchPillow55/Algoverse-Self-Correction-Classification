To solve the problem, we need to determine how many wheels Henry will use to assemble all the cars and motorcycles, and then find out how many wheels will be left after the assembly.

### Step 1: Calculate the total number of wheels needed for the cars.

Henry needs 4 wheels for each car. He has 57 cars, so the total number of wheels needed for the cars is calculated as follows:

\[
\text{Wheels for cars} = \text{Number of cars} \times \text{Wheels per car}
\]
\[
\text{Wheels for cars} = 57 \times 4 = 228
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the total number of wheels needed for the motorcycles.

Henry needs 2 wheels for each motorcycle. He has 73 motorcycles, so the total number of wheels needed for the motorcycles is calculated as follows:

\[
\text{Wheels for motorcycles} = \text{Number of motorcycles} \times \text{Wheels per motorcycle}
\]
\[
\text{Wheels for motorcycles} = 73 \times 2 = 146
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the total number of wheels needed for both cars and motorcycles.

Now, we add the wheels needed for the cars and the motorcycles to find the total number of wheels required:

\[
\text{Total wheels needed} = \text{Wheels for cars} + \text{Wheels for motorcycles}
\]
\[
\text{Total wheels needed} = 228 + 146 = 374
\]

### Step 4: Calculate the number of wheels left after assembly.

Henry starts with 650 wheels. To find out how many wheels will be left after he assembles the toys, we subtract the total number of wheels needed from the total number of wheels he has:

\[
\text{Wheels left} = \text{Total wheels} - \text{Total wheels needed}
\]
\[
\text{Wheels left} = 650 - 374 = 276
\]

### Final Answer

After assembling all the toys, Henry will have **276 wheels left**.
