To find out how much it will cost to fill the Smith family's swimming pool, we need to follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the volume of the pool in cubic feet.**
2. **Convert the volume from cubic feet to gallons.**
3. **Calculate the total cost to fill the pool based on the cost per gallon.**

### Step 1: Calculate the Volume of the Pool

The volume \( V \) of a rectangular pool can be calculated using the formula:

\[
V = \text{length} \times \text{width} \times \text{depth}
\]

Given:
- Length = 25 feet
- Width = 14 feet
- Depth = 4 feet

Substituting the values into the formula:

\[
V = 25 \, \text{ft} \times 14 \, \text{ft} \times 4 \, \text{ft}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. Calculate the area of the base (length Ã— width):
   \[
   25 \times 14 = 350 \, \text{square feet}
   \]

2. Now multiply the area by the depth:
   \[
   350 \, \text{sq ft} \times 4 \, \text{ft} = 1400 \, \text{cubic feet}
   \]

So, the volume of the pool is \( 1400 \, \text{cubic feet} \).

### Step 2: Convert Cubic Feet to Gallons

The pool company instructed the Smiths to multiply the volume by 5.9 to find the number of gallons needed. Therefore, we calculate:

\[
\text{Gallons} = 1400 \, \text{cubic feet} \times 5.9
\]

Calculating this:

\[
\text{Gallons} = 1400 \times 5.9 = 8260 \, \text{gallons}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Total Cost to Fill the Pool

The cost to fill the pool is given as $0.10 per gallon. Therefore, we calculate the total cost \( C \) as follows:

\[
C = \text{Gallons} \times \text{Cost per gallon}
\]

Substituting the values we have:

\[
C = 8260 \, \text{gallons} \times 0.10 \, \text{dollars/gallon}
\]

Calculating this:

\[
C = 8260 \times 0.10 = 826 \, \text{dollars}
\]

### Final Answer

The total cost to fill the Smith family's swimming pool is **$826**.
