To determine how many classes Manny can miss before his parents decide not to sign him up again, we need to analyze the cost per class based on the total cost and the number of classes he attends.

1. **Total Cost of Classes**: Manny signed up for Karate classes for a total of $60.

2. **Total Number of Classes**: There are 10 classes in total.

3. **Cost Per Class Calculation**: 
   - If Manny attends all 10 classes, the cost per class would be:
     \[
     \text{Cost per class} = \frac{\text{Total Cost}}{\text{Total Classes}} = \frac{60}{10} = 6 \text{ dollars per class}
     \]
   - This is below the $10 threshold set by his parents.

4. **Understanding the Condition**: His parents have stated that if the cost per class exceeds $10, they won't sign him up again. Therefore, we need to find out how many classes he can miss while still keeping the cost per class at or below $10.

5. **Let \( x \) be the number of classes Manny misses**. 
   - This means he will attend \( 10 - x \) classes.
   - The new cost per class, if he misses \( x \) classes, will be:
     \[
     \text{New Cost per class} = \frac{\text{Total Cost}}{\text{Classes Attended}} = \frac{60}{10 - x}
     \]

6. **Setting Up the Inequality**: We want to ensure that the new cost per class does not exceed $10:
   \[
   \frac{60}{10 - x} \leq 10
   \]

7. **Solving the Inequality**:
   - Multiply both sides by \( 10 - x \) (noting that \( 10 - x > 0 \) since he cannot miss all classes):
     \[
     60 \leq 10(10 - x)
     \]
   - Expanding the right side:
     \[
     60 \leq 100 - 10x
     \]
   - Rearranging the inequality:
     \[
     10x \leq 100 - 60
     \]
     \[
     10x \leq 40
     \]
     \[
     x \leq 4
     \]

8. **Conclusion**: Manny can miss up to 4 classes. If he misses 4 classes, he will attend \( 10 - 4 = 6 \) classes, and the cost per class will be:
   \[
   \text{Cost per class} = \frac{60}{6} = 10 \text{ dollars per class}
   \]
   If he misses 5 classes, he will attend only 5 classes, and the cost per class will be:
   \[
   \text{Cost per class} = \frac{60}{5} = 12 \text{ dollars per class}
   \]
   which exceeds the $10 limit.

Thus, the maximum number of classes Manny can miss before his parents decide not to sign him up again is:

\[
\boxed{4}
\]
